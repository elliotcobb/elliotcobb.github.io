Bikes for the people!

January 13, 2025

Loved this Mother Jones article about Denver's e-bike subsidy programs

It elucitated things I've been thinking about for a while, like:

  • Why do we have electric car subsidies, but not e-bike subsidies? Bikes are way cheaper and even more energy efficient. I guess most people don't have safe bike infrastructure, though.
  • Biking is just joyful
    • Great exercise
    • It's a little aventure
    • You see your neighbors
    • Self reliance and agency - it's just you powering yourself around
    • Kids can bike too!
    • Never stuck in traffic
    • Parking is a breeze
    • You can smell the flowers
    • You learn how to navigate your city
    • You're never worried about accidentally mauling a pedestrian
  • When people bike they are making the city safer, quieter and cleaner versus if they were in a car. Why wouldn't we want to incentivize that?
  • Here's to hoping more bikers leads to more bike lane advocates and more bike lanes leads to even more bikers...
  • Let's build roads that our kids and grandparents feel safe using! After all, a full 30% of Americans cannot or do not drive.
